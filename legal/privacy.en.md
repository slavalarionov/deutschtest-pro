## 2. What data we collect

### 2.1. Data you provide directly

**At registration:**
- email address;
- name (optional);
- password (stored only as a salted hash; we never see your plaintext password);
- preferred interface language.

**During use:**
- your responses to tasks (text in Schreiben, voice recordings and their transcripts in Sprechen);
- results and history of completed Modules;
- responses to optional surveys (e.g., "How did you hear about us?");
- feedback after Module completion (optional).

**At payment:**
- We do **not** receive or store your payment card data. This is handled directly by the payment provider Prodamus.
- We receive a payment confirmation, transaction ID, and amount from the payment provider.

### 2.2. Data collected automatically

- **Technical data:** IP address, browser type and version, operating system, language, time zone — used for service operation and abuse prevention.
- **Session cookies:** technically necessary cookies for keeping you logged in. Without these, the service can't function.
- **Anonymous statistics:** aggregate site usage via the **Umami** analytics tool, which doesn't use cookies and doesn't identify individual users.

### 2.3. Data we do NOT collect

- We don't use advertising cookies or tracking pixels.
- We don't deploy Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, or similar tracking systems (as of this version).
- We don't access your microphone without your explicit permission (only during active Sprechen modules with your consent).

## 7. Your rights under GDPR

You have the following rights:

- **Right of access** (Art. 15) — find out what data we hold about you;
- **Right to rectification** (Art. 16) — correct inaccurate data;
- **Right to erasure** (Art. 17) — request deletion ("right to be forgotten"). You can delete most data yourself via account settings;
- **Right to restriction** (Art. 18) — pause certain processing;
- **Right to data portability** (Art. 20) — receive your data in a machine-readable format;
- **Right to object** (Art. 21) — object to processing based on legitimate interests;
- **Right to withdraw consent** — at any time, with effect for the future;
- **Right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority** (Art. 77) — file a complaint with your country's data protection authority.

**To exercise any of these rights**, email us at **hallo@deutschtest.pro** with the subject line "Data Protection Request". We respond within 30 days.

## 8. Data security

We apply reasonable technical and organizational measures:

- HTTPS/TLS encryption for data in transit;
- passwords stored only as salted hashes;
- restricted database access at the infrastructure level;
- regular backups with automatic deletion of old copies;
- role-based access controls.

In case of a data breach, we'll notify you and the supervisory authority within the GDPR's 72-hour deadline (Art. 33 GDPR).
